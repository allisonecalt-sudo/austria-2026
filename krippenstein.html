Wed-night summit — what we considered + why we pivoted away

This page used to be the live comparison hub for a Wed-night summit-stay slot. As of Mon May 18 2026, that slot is gone — the trip was restructured to drop the summit overnight entirely. The short version. What started as one mountain-summit hotel pick (Schafbergspitze) became a swap (Lodge am Krippenstein), then a 5-option menu when Krippenstein went full, then finally got cut entirely when Avital proposed a cleaner trip shape: 2 nights Zell am See + 2 nights Wolfgangsee + 1 night airport hotel, with the Schafberg cog kept as a day-trip only. Four iterations across roughly two weeks. We're not sleeping on a summit on this trip.

The journey, in four moves

1 · Rejected

Original plan: Schafbergspitze summit hotel

The first pick was Schafbergspitze — the historic mountain hotel at 1,783m at the top of the Schafberg cog from St. Wolfgang. The concept was right (sleep on a summit, sunset + sunrise from a platform you can't get to without the hotel) — but the reviews didn't hold up. When Allison asked the open-ended question "is it creepy, what do they say online," the cross-check surfaced a 3.6★ Google rating across 1,012 reviews, with persistent flags about basic 2-star rooms, cash-preferred reception, and rude staff. The pattern wasn't a few bad nights — it was structural.

Verdict: rejected. Schafbergspitze runbook kept at schafbergspitze.html as a superseded reference. The mountain stays on the trip as a day-trip option — the cog rates 4.5+ and the view is real. We're just not sleeping there.

2 · Blocked

Pivot 1: Lodge am Krippenstein → went full

The swap was Lodge am Krippenstein — a modern architectural lodge at 2,063m on the Dachstein plateau, 9.2/10 across 339 Booking reviews, in our actual valley (10-min drive from the Obertraun base, then two cable cars up). Better elevation, better reviews, takes cards, books online, 5 Fingers cantilevered platform ~15 min walk for sunset. A bilingual EN+DE inquiry email to Harald (stay@lodge.at) was drafted in Austrian-formal register, ready to send.

Verdict: blocked. On a live recheck Mon May 18, the lodge was FULL for Wed Jul 29 → Thu Jul 30 on Booking.com. The email never went out because the room availability collapsed first.

3 · Shipped

Pivot 2: 5-option lake/elevated menu

With Krippenstein gone, the response was breadth instead of depth. A 5-option menu of equally-good Wed-night candidates was researched, verified live on Booking.com 2026-05-17, and shipped as this page's content (commit 0ef43d3):

  • Lodge am Krippenstein (kept as 1-of-5, in case availability changed) — €124-€268
  • Seehotel am Hallstättersee — lake-level 4-star in Obertraun town — €128-€452
  • Post am See, Traunkirchen — 4-star Superior, infinity pool — €409
  • Brandauer's Villen, Strobl — boutique restored Wolfgangsee villa — €314-€397
  • Scalaria Sunset Wing, St. Wolfgang — west-facing wing, named for the sunset — €352-€391

Verdict: superseded the same week. The menu was solid — five different concepts, all bookable, all free-cancellation, full deep-dive for Krippenstein preserved at the bottom — but Avital read it as a sign the summit-overnight slot itself was the problem, not which hotel to pick.

4 · Current

Pivot 3: Avital's structural counter-proposal (Sun May 17)

Sunday May 17 evening, Avital wrote four notes in the trip app and sent two voice notes (23:25 + 23:26). The proposal wasn't "pick option 3 from the menu" — it was "drop the summit overnight entirely; restructure the trip shape." She put it plainly:

"From what I can see there's only that one hotel that's like specifically like this amazing thing on the cog — but it also seems like quite impractical… we could just stay at a hotel in the airport and then not have to worry about returning the car in the morning." — Avital, voice note, Sun May 17 23:25
"From what I can see the cog place is specific but not so practical. Seems like we can get other beautiful sunsets not staying there." — Avital, app note, Sun May 17 20:18

The proposed shape, which Allison accepted Mon May 18 ~12:21:

  • Sat-Sun: 2 nights Salzburg (unchanged)
  • Sun Jul 26 → Tue Jul 28: 2 nights Zell am See (replaces Obertraun)
  • Tue Jul 28 → Thu Jul 30: 2 nights Wolfgangsee region (St. Gilgen / St. Wolfgang)
  • Thu Jul 30 → Fri Jul 31: 1 night airport hotel + return rental car Thursday night (Avital's specific logistics call — no morning car-return scramble)
  • Fri Jul 31: fly out (unchanged)

Schafberg cog stays as a day-trip option — the mountain itself rates 4.5+, only the overnight summit hotel was the problem. No overnight on any summit this trip.

Six room categories — what to ask for

The lodge has six tiers from dorm bunks up to a full Panorama Suite. Beds are Zirben (Swiss stone pine) — a real Alpine tradition with claimed sleep-quality benefits. For two adults wanting the view, the sweet spot is a Gallery Room or Panorama Room.

5 Fingers — the sunset platform

A cantilevered viewing deck on the same plateau as the lodge — five steel "fingers" jutting straight out over the cliff. Omni-view across Hallstättersee, the painted village far below, and the full Dachstein massif behind. ~15 minutes' easy walk from the lodge.

For Wed Jul 29 sunset (~20:48 in this region), be on the platform by ~20:15 to catch the last gold. Bring a windproof layer — the wind on the plateau is real, even in July. After sunset, walk back to the lodge in dusk and have dinner.

The plateau also has the Welterbespirale (heritage spiral viewpoint) and several easy paths. The lodge offers free snowshoe + avalanche kit rental in winter — not relevant in July, but tells you what kind of place this is.

The 7 steps, in order

1. Book the room (this week)
Easiest path: Booking.com, instant confirm. Or send the email template below to stay@lodge.at. Ask for Gallery Room or Panorama Room. 2 adults, Wed Jul 29 → Thu Jul 30 (1 night).
2. Pack one small overnight bag each
Sleeping clothes, fresh layer for sunset + sunrise (it's ~10°C cooler than the valley at 2,063m, plateau wind), camera. Leave main suitcases in the car at the cable car valley station — there's parking right there in Obertraun.
3. Pack kosher dinner + breakfast
Bring BYO food up — sealed kosher meat (Vienna Va'ad or Israel-brought), bread, fruit, breakfast for the morning. The lodge does have a kitchen/restaurant but not kosher; some guests may opt in for breakfast as a non-kosher dairy/coffee option (your call — ask at booking if you want to drop the lodge breakfast and reduce the rate).
4. Drive Obertraun apartment → cable car valley station
~10 min drive. Same valley as the 3-night Obertraun base — this is the "zero detour" part. Cable car operator: Dachstein Salzkammergut Bergbahnen. Operation times →
5. Ride the cable car up (two stages)
Cable car runs every 15 minutes in summer. First UP 08:40, last UP around 18:45, last DOWN 19:10. Buy lodge-guest tickets at the valley counter (lodge will confirm whether included in the rate). Two gondola stages get you to the Krippenstein summit station at 2,063m. ~10 min walk on the plateau to the lodge.
6. Check in + sunset at 5 Fingers
Drop the bag at the lodge. Walk ~15 min on the plateau to the 5 Fingers platform. Sunset Wed Jul 29 ~20:48 — omni-view over Hallstättersee and the Dachstein. After the last gondola down at 19:10, the plateau empties out and it's just overnight guests. Dinner BYO at the lodge, Zirben-pine beds, stars without light pollution.
7. Sunrise + descent (Thu morning)
Sunrise Thu Jul 30 ~05:30 — first light on the Dachstein face. Worth setting an alarm. Breakfast (BYO or lodge), pack out. First cable car down at 08:40. Drive ~1h15 to the airport-area apartment (last night before the Fri morning flight).

What could have gone wrong (preserved risk register)

Miss the last cable car UP. Summer last UP is around 18:45. If you arrive later, you need the lodge's late-check-in instruction (ask in advance — there may be a service gondola for guests). Padding > scrambling.
Weather closes the cable car. High winds can close the Dachstein Seilbahn. Lodge will refund or rebook. Plan B = sleep extra night at the Obertraun base and lose the summit night.
Low cloud at sunset. Even if the cable car runs, plateau can be in cloud. Sunrise is typically clearer than sunset in July (less afternoon convective build-up) so it's almost-impossible to lose both moments.
Forgetting the kosher dinner. No kosher option on the mountain. If you arrive without packed food, you're eating room snacks. Pre-pack at the Obertraun apartment Wed morning.
