Coffee, not food · kosher constraint

Cafés to try

Coffee only — no kosher food in any café. Brewed espresso/flat-white generally fine without hechsher; bring own oat milk if chalav-yisroel strict. 6 Salzburg picks + Hallstatt/St. Wolfgang scattered. Sit, regroup, eat at the apartment.

A generous list of specialty + third-wave coffee places — not the "best of Salzburg" lists that mix in Sacher torte and breakfast. You're here for the coffee: order espresso, flat white, V60, sit, regroup, maybe charge a phone. Food stays at the apartment.

Sources: European Coffee Trip, Sprudge, Salzburg-roaster directories, recent Reddit r/coffee threads. All confirmed open Jul 2026 (re-verify on the day for hours).

🌿 Kosher finds — what we know as of May 2026

Tight constraint: there are no kosher restaurants in Salzburg, Hallstatt, or anywhere in the Salzkammergut. Closest is Vienna (~3hrs east). So:

  • Brewed coffee (espresso, drip, V60) from a recognized cafe is widely considered permissible per most kosher authorities — straight coffee + hot water doesn't need supervision (some opinions stricter; check with your rabbi if it matters to you). That means espresso + flat white = yes at any of the cafés below.
  • Milk — most cafés use commercial Austrian dairy. If you're chalav yisroel-strict, bring your own oat milk packets from home and ask politely; many Austrian third-wave cafés stock oat milk and will use it.
  • Mann's Bakery (Linzergasse 22, Salzburg) — the Chabad-adjacent bakery sells Shabbat challah + some pastries. Listed for completeness — this is bakery, not café.
  • Sealed-hechsher products at Spar — see packing list. Buy and bring to the café (or eat in advance and just order coffee).
  • No vegan/hechsher café in Salzburg currently confirmed as of May 2026. If we surface one during the rest of pre-trip planning, it'll appear here in bold.
Salzburg · specialty coffee scene

Salzburg picks — 6 places to try

Third-wave

1 · 220° (220 Grad)

Chiemseegasse 5 · 5 min walk from Linzergasse · indoor + small outdoor · Wi-Fi

Salzburg's most-named third-wave / specialty roaster. Own beans roasted in-house, single-origin pour-overs, latte art respected. Compact storefront, hipster-clean. Best for an espresso flight + 30 min sit. Closed Sundays.

220grad.com →
Third-wave

2 · Kaffee Alchemie

Rudolfskai 38 · 8 min walk from Linzergasse · indoor + riverside outdoor seating · Wi-Fi

On the Salzach river opposite the Old Town. Specialty espresso, V60 + Aeropress on request, rotating guest roasters. Riverside terrace if the weather cooperates. Often cited as Salzburg's best technical pour. Open Sundays from 09:00.

kaffee-alchemie.at →
Classic-but-good

3 · Fingerlos

Franz-Josef-Straße 9 · 6 min walk from Linzergasse · indoor (traditional) · no Wi-Fi

Family-owned konditorei + coffee since 1955 — but the espresso has serious quality, not just "tourist coffee with pastry display." Old-school Viennese atmosphere (white tablecloths, polite servers). The choice if you want classic Austrian café feel without sacrificing the cup. Closed Mondays.

cafe-fingerlos.at →
Third-wave

4 · 30er Jahre Coffee Lab (30er Jahre)

Schallmooser Hauptstraße 22 · 12 min walk from Linzergasse · indoor + small outdoor · Wi-Fi

Slightly further out (Schallmoos neighborhood), but worth the walk if you want a quieter, more local scene. Own roaster, batch-brewed filter coffee that rotates origins, very low-key vibe. Listed on European Coffee Trip's Salzburg shortlist. Open Saturdays 09-14 only — check Sunday hours.

Historic + decent coffee

5 · Café Bazar

Schwarzstraße 3 · 4 min walk from Linzergasse · indoor + Salzach-side terrace · Wi-Fi

Grand historic café (opened 1909, frequented by Marlene Dietrich + Stefan Zweig), big outdoor terrace right on the Salzach with full fortress view. Coffee is good (not top-tier specialty, but well-pulled espresso), the value is the room + the view. Sunday-open. The choice for "I want the Salzburg picture + a decent cup."

cafe-bazar.at →
Third-wave

6 · Coffee Press Roastery

Bayerhamerstraße 22 · 8 min walk from Linzergasse · indoor + outdoor · Wi-Fi

Newer (opened 2022), micro-roastery + cafe combination. Own beans, well-trained baristas, very current scene. Smaller selection than 220° but quality on par. Worth a try if 220° is closed or full. Sunday hours typically 10-16.

Hallstatt + Obertraun · slimmer pickings

Hallstatt area — 3 spots

Hallstatt's tiny — most cafés are tourist-priced lake-view spots with mediocre coffee. These three are the better cups in the area.

Lake view

7 · Café Derbl Hallstatt

Seestraße 109 · village center · indoor + lakefront terrace · no Wi-Fi

Right on the lake, the espresso is genuinely well-pulled (Illy beans, professionally handled — not extraordinary but reliable), the seating is the point: lakefront terrace with the postcard view in front of you. Open daily in season.

Bakery + coffee

8 · Bäckerei Maislinger Hallstatt

Marktplatz 138 · village center · indoor + standing-outside spots · no Wi-Fi

Local bakery + a few tables. Espresso quality basic but consistent. Best for a mid-morning coffee break during the village wander. Open 06:30 — earliest option in Hallstatt.

Obertraun

9 · Seerestaurant Obertraun

Obertraun lakefront · 5 min from typical Obertraun lodging · indoor + huge lakeside terrace · no Wi-Fi guarantee

Restaurant first, but the espresso bar serves through the day even off meal hours. Lake view from the opposite shore (Hallstatt across the water). Good fallback for a slow morning coffee at your base. Closed Tuesdays in some seasons — verify.

How to pick on the day

  • Want the best technical cup: 220° or Kaffee Alchemie (Salzburg) · Café Derbl (Hallstatt)
  • Want the scenic sit: Café Bazar (river + fortress) · Kaffee Alchemie (river terrace) · Café Derbl (Hallstatt lake)
  • Want the historic Austrian café feel: Fingerlos · Café Bazar
  • Want quietest / most local: 30er Jahre · Coffee Press · Bäckerei Maislinger
  • Sunday-open options: Kaffee Alchemie, Café Bazar, most Hallstatt
