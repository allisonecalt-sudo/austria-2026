Late-July Salzkammergut + Bavarian Alps run 18-26°C daytime, ~12 rain days per month, mostly afternoon thunderstorms. Expect at least 1-2 wet days in 7. These are the all-weather swaps — indoor, partly-indoor, or rain-stays-pretty options that hold their value even soaked.

Check forecast night before: salzburg.info weather or ZAMG (national service). The Alps create their own micro-weather — a wet Salzburg morning often clears at Königssee by noon.

Per-day swaps

If it rains on...

Day 1 · Fri Jul 24 · Salzburg arrival

Default = Old Town wander + Mozart Geburtshaus · Plan C = stay indoor

Swap: Mozart Geburtshaus (already indoor, €13.50) → add DomQuartier (Cathedral + Residenz palace + Long Gallery, €13, 2 hours indoor) → café break → apartment by 16:00. Mirabell Gardens is still doable in light rain — covered arcade sections.

Day 2 · Sat Jul 25 · Shabbat in Salzburg

No swap needed — Shabbat is mostly indoor anyway

Shabbat day in the apartment + walks to/from Chabad. Rain just means a coat. If you do an afternoon stroll, the Salzach river walk under umbrella is still beautiful and covered along sections.

Day 3 · Sun Jul 26 · Move + Hallstatt

Default = drive to Obertraun + Hallstatt village · Plan C = drive + indoor

Swap: Drive to Obertraun (closed-car time is fine), then go straight to Hallstatt Salt Mine (Salzwelten Hallstatt) — funicular up to the mine entrance, full underground tour, indoor cafe at the top with panoramic view through glass. The salt mine is 90 min + you stay dry. Save lake walking for Day 4 or 5 if weather clears.

Salzwelten Hallstatt →

Day 4 · Mon Jul 27 · Königssee day (Germany)

Default = electric boat to St. Bartholomä + Obersee · Plan C = stay in Austria

Swap: Königssee boats run rain-or-shine but the magic dies in heavy rain (no sunset, no reflections). Two options: (a) Salzwelten Salzburg salt mine on the way back if you've already left Obertraun (Hallein, 30 min south of Salzburg, €25, 90 min underground), or (b) Werfen Eisriesenwelt ice cave (45 min south of Salzburg, €36, 75-min cave tour at 1°C inside — bring layers, rain outside is irrelevant). Both stay dry. Hohenwerfen Fortress combines well with the ice cave for a full indoor day.

Eisriesenwelt →

Day 5 · Tue Jul 28 · Hallstatt village + Five Fingers

Default = lake-front + Krippenstein cable car · Plan C = mine + village

Swap: Skip the Krippenstein cable car (Five Fingers viewpoint is useless in fog/rain — the operators close it when visibility drops). Do the Hallstatt Salt Mine in the morning instead, then the village itself (the lakefront stays pretty in mist — possibly more so), café break under an umbrella, and the Bone House at the Catholic church (small indoor stop, €2, weirdly memorable). If sun returns afternoon, go up Krippenstein then.

Salzwelten Hallstatt →

Day 6 · Wed Jul 29 · Gosausee + Schafberg

Default = lake walk + cogwheel railway · Plan C = castle + apartment day

Swap: Schafberg cogwheel railway closes in storms. Gosausee is beautiful in mist but mountains hide. Best swap: Hohenwerfen Fortress (50 min from Obertraun toward Salzburg — falconry shows happen even in light rain, indoor courtyards + interior rooms ~2 hours dry) + café + early return to apartment. If it's a full-day downpour, embrace the rest day — kitchen cooking, book reading, balcony if you have one, evening sauna at the Dachstein-Therme nearby.

Day 7 · Thu Jul 30 · Final day Obertraun → airport

Default = Hallstatt morning + drive back · Plan C = mine + drive

Swap: Salt mine morning (if you haven't done it), early lunch in Hallstatt under cover, then drive back via the autobahn (~1h15 to airport area). The drive itself is a non-issue in rain — Austrian autobahns drain well. Just don't push sunset spots; eat at the apartment, sleep early, 04:00 wake for 05:00 flight.

All-weather staples

6 things that hold their magic in rain

If you need an emergency swap on any day, these all stay good (or better) wet:

1 · Hallstatt Salt Mine (Salzwelten)

€41 incl. funicular · 90 min · indoor at 8°C

Underground slides, salt lake, mining history, panoramic glass platform at the entrance. Reach by funicular straight from village. The single best rainy-day option in the area.

Salzwelten →

2 · Eisriesenwelt ice cave (Werfen)

€36 incl. cable car · 75 min cave · 1°C inside (layers!)

World's largest ice cave. Cable car up, short hike to entrance (rain-resistant if you've got a jacket), then 75 min indoor cave tour with lanterns. Combine with Hohenwerfen Fortress at the base for a full day.

Eisriesenwelt →

3 · Hohenwerfen Fortress

€18 · 2-3 hours · partly indoor + covered courtyards

Falconry shows daily 11:15 + 15:15 (run in light rain), Princes' Chambers indoor tours, panoramic clifftop. Combine with Eisriesenwelt for a full all-weather day.

Hohenwerfen →

4 · DomQuartier Salzburg

€13 · 2 hours · 5 connected indoor sites

Cathedral, Residenz palace, Long Gallery, Franciscan Church — all connected by indoor passages. Walk between them without going outside. Best for a rainy Salzburg afternoon.

DomQuartier →

5 · Dachstein-Therme Bad Goisern

€19 day pass · sauna + indoor pool · 20 min from Obertraun

Spa complex with indoor + outdoor pools (the outdoor stays open in rain, kind of wonderful actually), sauna, steam room. Perfect for a recovery day mid-week.

Dachstein-Therme →

6 · Apartment day with cooking

Free · all day · zero stress

Both Salzburg and Obertraun apartments have full kitchens. A rainy day with quinoa salad prep, slow morning, a long book, balcony coffee in your sweater — this is also vacation. Don't over-schedule the swap; sometimes Plan C is "stay in."

Read the sky

Salzkammergut weather patterns

  • Mornings often clear, afternoons often storm. Schedule outdoor priority for 09:00-14:00, indoor for 14:00-19:00 if forecast looks unstable.
  • Königssee + Hallstatt sit in valleys — sun there even when Salzburg is grey. Don't cancel based on Salzburg radar alone.
  • Mountain weather is local. Krippenstein, Schafberg, Eagle's Nest can all be in cloud while the lakeside below is fine. Cable cars publish daily status — check before driving up.
  • Late July thunderstorms are short. Wait 30-60 min and they often pass. Don't bail on a day for an afternoon shower.
