The idea that makes it work

Why it's “like milk”

חלב סתם

“We rely on milk because it's so watched.”

That's the whole backbone — and you named it. Chalav stam (Rav Moshe Feinstein): regular European milk is trusted not because a rabbi stood there, but because government regulation is so tight that non-kosher milk basically can't get in. Austria is EU, so the OU's own poskim apply it here.

Ingredient-based kashrut extends that logic to labels: EU law forces full, honest ingredient disclosure — so the label becomes the thing you trust, the way you trust the milk. Most Modern-Orthodox rely on chalav stam; a stricter minority uses only chalav yisrael — for them, uncertified dairy is out and plant-based is the answer.

Path 2 · The in-aisle tool

German label lookup

See a word on the ingredient list (Zutaten) and not sure? Type it. The real work is spotting a handful of red words fast — everything else is mostly fine.

Fastest check in the store

Snap it & ask

In the aisle, photograph the ingredient list and paste this with the pic — you get an instant read:

“Read this German ingredient list. Flag any of: Gelatine, Karmin/Cochenille, Molke/Milcheiweiß, Schweineschmalz/tierisches Fett, tierisches Lab, Käse, Wein/Weinessig/Traubensaft/Balsamico, Aroma. Tell me if it's dairy, pareve, or vegan.”

Your example, worked out

Yogurt & the “Icelandic” one

Plain dairy yogurt

Yogurt is set by bacterial culture, not rennet — so no cheese problem. If you hold by chalav stam, plain Naturjoghurt is workable by ingredient. The whole game is the label:

  • Plain Natur / ungesüßt dodges Aroma, fruit prep & carmine
  • Gelatine in creamy/low-fat ones — stop
  • Karmin in pink/red fruit ones — stop
  • Aroma / Fruchtzubereitung — the judgment call
  • Skyr is the exception — sometimes a little rennet, a bigger question mark

The non-dairy play

Your instinct is dead right: no milk = no milk problem at all, and it works for a chalav-yisrael-only person too. A clean plant label is basically the whole question — and Alpro plant milks are on Misrachi's certified list (KLBD).

On the “made in Iceland” one: couldn't confirm a non-dairy yogurt actually made in Iceland on Austrian shelves — the real one is Icelandic Provisions oat-milk skyr, a US product. 📸 Photograph the tub if you spot it. What you'll find in Austria:

  • Alpro — soy & coconut (KLBD-certified per Misrachi)
  • Joya · SPAR Veggie · dmBio — plant, plain
  • Still scan for Aroma & thickeners on the tub
