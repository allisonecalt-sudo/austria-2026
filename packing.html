Packing — what we bring

Same template as Montenegro 2024 — that worked, repeat it. Apartments have kitchens, so cooking is the default. No kosher restaurant in the entire Salzkammergut / Salzburg region (per Chabad Salzburg verified May 2026), so shelf-stable from home is the spine, fresh produce + sealed-hechsher dairy locally.

From Israel — shelf-stable food

Allison's verified Montenegro pack — known good for 10 days, 2 people. Scale slightly down for 7 days.

  • Instant coffee (a lot — Allison drinks ~5 cups/day)
  • Pita (vacuum-sealed) — 2-3 packs
  • Crackers — assorted boxes
  • Oatmeal packets — for hike breakfasts
  • Pasta — 2 boxes
  • Quinoa — 2 bags (Avital's signature salad base)
  • Pasta sauce in jars — 2-3
  • Protein bars + granola bars — generous
  • Dried fruits + nuts — for car snacks and summit picnics
  • Rice cakes
  • Tuna packets/cans — 4-5
  • Salami (vacuum-sealed kosher) — 2 packs
  • Beef jerky
  • Chickpeas (canned or instant)
  • Tehina jar
  • Almond butter
  • Mayo (small)
  • Salt + pepper grinder

Buy locally at Spar / Billa / Hofer

  • Cooking oil (any) — first stop on Friday afternoon
  • Fresh produce — cucumbers, tomatoes, peppers, avocado, fruit (no kashrut needed, wash + check)
  • Sealed-hechsher dairy — Kosher Tirol supplies Tirol/Salzburg Spars. Look for the hechsher; confirm symbols before buying.
  • Bread / challah — Mann's Bakery (Linzergasse 22, Salzburg) for Shabbat challah backup. Otherwise sealed packaged bread from Spar.
  • Plastic disposables — paper plates, plastic cutlery, paper towels
  • Cleaning wipes for the kitchen
  • Bottled water — Austria tap is excellent but trail water bottles + a 6-pack for picnics

Kitchen kit from home

Same as Montenegro — apartments have basics but not kosher-separated.

  • Small dairy pan
  • Dairy spatula
  • Meat pan
  • Dairy pot
  • Cutting board
  • Knives (good ones — kosher rentals never have these)
  • Bowls (lightweight)
  • Metal spoon
  • Induction plate decision: Verify stove type with each apartment confirmation. master Linzergasse + Haus Edelweiss + morand both list "kitchen" without specifying — message host. If induction, bring a universal induction plate; if gas or electric coil, leave it home.

Austria-specific additions

  • Fleece / midlayer — Eisriesenwelt ice cave is below freezing year-round; Schafberg summit is ~12°C even at sunset in July.
  • Rain shell — Salzkammergut summer afternoons can flip to rain in 30 min (see: Black Lake storm).
  • Hiking shoes — not boots, but real shoes. Crocs for the apartment and the lake.
  • Swimsuit + quick-dry towel — Wolfgangsee swim, possible Lake Hallstatt dip.
  • Sun hat + sunscreen — boats on the Königssee are open-top.
  • Power adapter — Austria uses Type F (Schuko, same as continental EU).
  • EU SIM or eSIM — Austria has good mobile coverage; useful for cave-ticket booking on the road.
  • Refillable water bottle — Allison's Owala. Tap water is clean.
  • Cash €100-200 — Austria is card-friendly but some mountain huts and small cog-railway snacks are cash-only.
  • Headlamp / small flashlight — for the ice cave if your guide's carbide lamp falters.

What we DON'T bring

  • Bug spray — Salzkammergut is colder + windier than Žabljak, much less of an issue.
  • Dressy clothes — no fancy dinners on this trip, Shabbat clothing only.
  • Heavy hiking gear — gondolas do the climbing, no overnight huts on this plan.
  • Print maps — phones work everywhere on this route.
