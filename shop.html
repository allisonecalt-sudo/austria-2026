By ingredient — the fallback

For when there's no certified option. Recognize it by the picture, see the ingredients in German and English, and see exactly what leniency it leans on. Try the certified page first — that's the easy path.

Gil has answered — and it opened things UP. (Allison's brother-in-law, who knows this well — informed input, not a psak.) ✅ Reading ingredients is approved in general — "no difference between a long list and a short list if you understand the ingredients." ✅ Whey (Molke) & milk powder are fine under chalav stam — that was red before, and it's in a lot of chocolate, baked goods and snacks. ✅ "May contain traces" — ignore it entirely. ✅ Tuna · chips & popcorn · canned vegetables · cold-smoked salmon · crackers, crispbread, pretzels & cereal (pas palter). Anything you cook from raw was never a question.
Two things that got stricter. 🔴 Skyr — skip it. Austria's food code classes it as a Frischkäse, so it's a cheese question, not a yogurt one. Plain Naturjoghurt instead. 🟡 Emulsifiers (E471 & co) — EU law never declares the origin, so the label can't answer it. A V-Label vegan mark settles it; otherwise ask whether the product would collapse without it. Still open: Aroma and instant couscous. Frozen fries are settled — Gil: part-cooked + you finish it = ok.
