Synagogues — yes, there are two

Allison\'s belief that there\'s no Chabad in Salzburg was wrong (good wrong). There are two minyan options, both walkable from any old-town apartment.

Chabad

Chabad of Salzburg

Linzergasse 76, 5020 Salzburg
Rabbi Menachem + Chani (warm hosts, run Shabbat meals)
WhatsApp: +43 676 8318 1555
chabadsalzburg.com

Shabbat meals to-go (Flowiz app). Book ~1 week ahead. Friday-night dinner is the move — Allison made a friend named Shaindy at Chabad Budva over how hot it was, that exact thing can happen here.

IKG

Israelitische Kultusgemeinde Salzburg

Lasserstrasse 8, 5020 Salzburg
Phone: +43 662 87 2228

ikg-salzburg.at

Services Friday evening + Shabbat morning per their listings. More traditional community-shul feel than Chabad. Worth calling ahead to confirm summer times.

Kosher food — the fail-loud truth

There is no kosher restaurant in Salzburg (per Chabad of Salzburg FAQ, verified May 2026). Closest organized kosher infrastructure is Vienna, ~3 hours by car. So the plan is:

  • Bring shelf-stable from Israel: kibbeh mix, instant rice cups, peanut butter, halva, tuna, instant soups, oatmeal, cous-cous, dried fruit, snack bars, instant coffee. Like Montenegro, but pre-stocked at the apartment Friday afternoon.
  • Buy fresh locally: raw produce (no kashrut needed — wash + check), sealed-hechsher dairy at Spar / Billa / Hofer. Kosher Tirol supplies Tirol/Salzburg Spars with a kosher department (wine, grape juice, dips, cheese). Confirm hechsher symbols before buying.
  • Chabad Friday night + lunch: the only prepared kosher meals. Book early.
  • Picnics every day: the way Avital fed both of us in Montenegro (quinoa, avocado, chickpeas, tomatoes, cucumbers + a sauce) generalizes. Apartment kitchen + cooler bag = lunch wherever you stop.

Neighborhood pick

Andräviertel (around Linzergasse, north side of Salzach river). Walking distance to Chabad (2 min from Linzergasse 76), 8 min to old town, 12 min to IKG. Lots of apartment-style rentals with kitchens.

Alternative: Mülln, slightly further but quieter, riverside paths, 15-min walk to Chabad. Cheaper. Less buzzy.

Walkable Shabbat plan

Within walking distance of Andräviertel (no driving, no eruv assumed unless verified — carry-rules conservative): Mirabell gardens (5 min), Mönchsberg stairs (10 min), the riverside path along the Salzach in both directions, the old town itself. Hohensalzburg fortress is up the hill but the walk from old town to the fortress crest is ~30 min on foot (no money needed if you skip the lift).
