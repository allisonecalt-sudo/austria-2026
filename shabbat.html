Kosher food — the fail-loud truth

No kosher restaurant in Salzburg. Chabad's own FAQ: "Unfortunately, there are currently no kosher restaurants in Salzburg Austria." Closest organized kosher restaurant infrastructure is Vienna, ~3 hours by car. So the food plan stacks three layers:

Layer 1 — Shelf-stable from Israel (the spine)

Full pack list on the packing page. Tuna, sealed crackers, olive oil, peanut butter, halva, instant coffee, tea, salt, hard candy, granola bars, a couple of cans of soup, sealed dry pasta + sauce. This is the Montenegro template.

Layer 2 — Local supermarkets for fresh + sealed-hechsher

Store Address What to buy
Spar Linzergasse 57 Linzergasse 17-19 / 57, 5020 Salzburg · ~3-min walk from apartment, ~2-min from Chabad Fresh produce, water, sealed dairy with hechsher, eggs
Interspar / Eurospar multiple locations (closest to old town: Schallmoos) Wider kosher-import selection than the small Spar — worth a single bigger run
Billa, Hofer (Aldi) across Salzburg Basic produce, sealed UHT milk; check every label

Hechshers to look for on Austrian dairy / packaged goods

  • BD (Beis Din) + OK — most common on European imports
  • OU — American but widely on imported snacks + cereals
  • KLBD (London Beis Din), Star-K, CRC
  • Kosher Tirol — Austria-specific (more on this below)
  • Vaad of Vienna / IKG Wien — local; trustworthy
  • For cheese: needs explicit hechsher (gelatin/rennet issue). Don't trust "vegetarian" labels.
  • For wine: needs explicit Mevushal/regular kosher hechsher. The Spar on Linzergasse will not have any — bring kiddush wine from Israel or order via Chabad/Kosher Tirol.

Kosher Tirol — Austria's kosher distributor

Verified real (koshertirol.com · info@koshertirol.com). They distribute basic kosher products — milk, yogurt, cheese, dips, bread, wine, grape juice, frozen meat, ready meals — to Mpreis chain stores across Tirol + Salzburg state and to "selected Spar" stores. Their summer-services page lists 15 Mpreis locations; none are in Salzburg city itself (closest is Altenmarkt and Flachau, ~45-75 min south). They also deliver anywhere in Austria. Email info@koshertirol.com ~2 weeks ahead to ask: (a) which Salzburg-city Spar (if any) carries their summer 2026 kosher section, and (b) whether they'll deliver kiddush wine + frozen kosher meat to the master Linzergasse apartment for Friday-afternoon arrival.

No eruv. No mikveh. Plan accordingly.

Chabad of Salzburg's own FAQ, verbatim: "There is no Eruv in Salzburg" and "Unfortunately, there are currently no active Mikvahs in Salzburg."

Practical consequences

  • Don't carry anything in public on Shabbat — no siddur in a purse, no tissues in a pocket if you're being strict, no keys (leave one inside the door, use a doorstop, or coordinate with host).
  • Wear, don't carry: sweater wrapped around shoulders is wearing; pushing the apartment key inside a sewn-closed shabbat-belt is wearing. Carrying a phone is carrying.
  • Apartment key trick: tell the host you'll be in/out throughout Shabbat. Either leave the door unlocked (master Linzergasse has a secure building entrance — apartment unlocked is usually fine) or arrange a hidden spot. Confirm with the apartment Friday afternoon.
  • Apartment-to-Chabad route is fully within a private domain transition? No — you'd walk public street, public sidewalk. Be carry-aware.
  • If you want to be lenient: Chabad-style poskim often treat a closed pocket with a non-essential item differently from a real "carry." Ask the rabbi if it matters to you. v1 of this page suggested asking; that's still the right move.

Jewish historical sites (post-Shabbat / for context)

Salzburg's pre-Holocaust community was destroyed in 1938. Today ~100 Jews. Worth knowing about, even if you don't visit:

  • Salzburg Synagogue (IKG) · Lasserstrasse 8 — original 1901 building, destroyed Nov 1938, rebuilt 1967-68 with restored historic façade. Currently active under IKG.
  • Jewish cemetery · Aigen district, southeast of city. Contact IKG (+43 662 87 2228) for access — usually closed; the office will arrange a visit.
  • Stolpersteine — bronze sidewalk markers for Jews murdered in the Holocaust, scattered throughout the old town and Andräviertel. You'll see them on the walk between apartment and Chabad.
