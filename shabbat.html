Kosher food — the fail-loud truth

There is no kosher restaurant in Salzburg or the Salzkammergut region (per Chabad of Salzburg FAQ, verified May 2026). Closest organized kosher infrastructure is Vienna, ~3 hours by car. So the plan is the Montenegro plan:

  • Bring shelf-stable from Israel — full list on the packing page. Pre-stock apartment Friday afternoon.
  • Buy fresh locally: Spar (closest one to master Linzergasse is on the same street — 2 min walk). Sealed-hechsher dairy is available via Kosher Tirol. Confirm hechsher symbols before buying.
  • Chabad Friday night + lunch — the only prepared kosher meals. Book early.
  • Picnics every day after Shabbat — Avital's quinoa/avocado/chickpea template, just scaled.

Walkable Shabbat plan

From master Linzergasse (Andräviertel side of the Salzach): Mirabell gardens (5 min), Mönchsberg stairs from the Staatsbrücke (10 min), the Salzach river paths both directions, the old town itself (8 min). Hohensalzburg fortress crest is ~30 min on foot via the Mönchsberg ridge — Shabbat-legal as long as you don't pay for the lift.

Saturday afternoon Plan A: Mönchsberg via Toscaninihof stairs → Modern Art Museum terrace (skip entry, just the viewing balcony) → ridge walk → back down to old town for the long European twilight. See itinerary Saturday card.

No eruv assumed in Salzburg. Carrying-rules conservative. Verify with Chabad if it matters.
