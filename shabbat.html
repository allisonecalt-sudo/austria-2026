Shabbat in Salzburg · Jul 24-25, 2026

CL 20:35 Fri · Havdalah 21:49 Sat. Sleep at Bergland Hotel (4-min walk to Chabad — leading pick; master Linzergasse listed below as backup). The shape that fits: meals at the Gruzman table both nights — WhatsApp Chani +43 676 8318 1555 by Jul 13. Backup: Flowiz to-go + apartment. Shelf-stable from Israel + Spar Linzergasse Fri AM. No eruv, no mikveh.

How to read this page: Shabbat is the most time-locked part of the week (candle-lighting, sunset, Havdalah are clock facts). The reframe is in WHERE you do it + WHAT you eat — we'd recommend the Gruzman table at Chabad both meals (warm, close, the Budva-Chabad pattern). Alternates: Flowiz to-go + apartment, or splitting (one at Chabad, one at home). IKG is the building, Chabad is the people — both walkable; pick.

Where we're sleeping for Shabbat

Bergland Hotel (Rupertgasse 15, leading pick as of 2026-05-19 PM) — 4-min walk to Chabad Salzburg (Linzergasse 76). 9.0 / 1,092 reviews. €226/night. Adults-only, family-run since 1912. No kitchen — the meals-at-Gruzman plan is the primary plan; the kitchen-required deep-planning text below is from the prior master Linzergasse pick (kept as a backup, see Stay page). If we swap to a kitchen-having pick (Junker's, master Linzergasse, Pension Elisabeth), the kitchen-prep planning re-activates as written.

Chabad of Salzburg — the primary play

Run by Rabbi Menachem Gruzman + Chani Gruzman. Their own FAQ confirms it's the only working Jewish hub for a Shabbat visitor — IKG is a building, Chabad is the people. They explicitly invite travelers but require advance contact.

Services they actually run

  • Friday-night davening + dinner at the Chabad House. Time not published online — confirm via WhatsApp 1-2 weeks ahead.
  • Shabbat morning Shacharit — likely ~9:30, confirm.
  • Shabbat kiddush + lunch after davening.
  • Daily prayers (their website lists "Daily Prayers" — confirm if a weekday minyan actually forms; Jewish-travel sites note it's not guaranteed).
  • Shabbat Meals To-Go via the Flowiz app — order ahead and pick up Friday afternoon if you want to eat at the apartment instead of at Chabad.

Reviews (Wheree + Jewish-travel blogs, verified May 2026)

Consistent praise: "heartwarming atmosphere," "friendly hospitality," "delicious and plentiful food," "feel at home". Multiple visitor reports say sitting at the Gruzman table is the highlight of a Salzburg trip. This is the Budva-Chabad pattern — a small house, a warm rebbetzin, strangers becoming a table. Budva-coded but real.

What to ASK them by WhatsApp this week

  1. "Can two women join Friday-night dinner + Shabbat lunch on July 24-25, 2026?" (yes is the default answer per their site, but lock the seat)
  2. "What time is Friday-night davening? Shabbat-morning Shacharit?"
  3. "What's the per-meal cost / donation? How do we pay?" (Flowiz handles meal-to-go pricing; on-site meals often donation-based — ASK)
  4. "Any women's-section seating note we should know — Orthodox mechitza setup?"
  5. "Where do you recommend buying kosher dairy + challah locally? Does any Spar in Salzburg carry Kosher Tirol products this summer?"
  6. "Confirm: no eruv, no mikveh — anything changed?" (their FAQ says NO to both)

Action item: WhatsApp Chani at +43 676 8318 1555 by Mon Jul 13 (~10 days before). State arrival date, that you're two women shomer-Shabbat, that you want BOTH meals + shul. She'll confirm. This is the only piece of the trip that requires human coordination — everything else is bookable through a screen.

IKG Salzburg — the official community shul (backup option)

Separate institution from Chabad. The historical Jewish community of Salzburg — ~100 members today, rebuilt the synagogue 1967-68 after the 1938 destruction.

Address Lasserstrasse 8, 5020 Salzburg (Schallmoos district, ~12 min walk from master Linzergasse)
Phone +43 662 87 2228
Email office@ikg-salzburg.at
Office hours Mon-Thu 09:00-15:00, Fri 09:00-13:00 (closed on Jewish + national holidays)
Website ikg-salzburg.at

Verdict: Chabad is the play. IKG is the building. If for some reason Chabad falls through, IKG is walkable from master Linzergasse — but call ahead Fri morning to confirm services are even running that week.

Kosher food — the fail-loud truth

No kosher restaurant in Salzburg. Chabad's own FAQ: "Unfortunately, there are currently no kosher restaurants in Salzburg Austria." Closest organized kosher restaurant infrastructure is Vienna, ~3 hours by car. So the food plan stacks three layers:

Layer 1 — Shelf-stable from Israel (the spine)

Full pack list on the packing page. Tuna, sealed crackers, olive oil, peanut butter, halva, instant coffee, tea, salt, hard candy, granola bars, a couple of cans of soup, sealed dry pasta + sauce.

Layer 2 — Local supermarkets for fresh + sealed-hechsher

Store Address What to buy
Spar Linzergasse 57 Linzergasse 17-19 / 57, 5020 Salzburg · ~3-min walk from apartment, ~2-min from Chabad Fresh produce, water, sealed dairy with hechsher, eggs
Interspar / Eurospar multiple locations (closest to old town: Schallmoos) Wider kosher-import selection than the small Spar — worth a single bigger run
Billa, Hofer (Aldi) across Salzburg Basic produce, sealed UHT milk; check every label

Hechshers to look for on Austrian dairy / packaged goods

  • BD (Beis Din) + OK — most common on European imports
  • OU — American but widely on imported snacks + cereals
  • KLBD (London Beis Din), Star-K, CRC
  • Kosher Tirol — Austria-specific (more on this below)
  • Vaad of Vienna / IKG Wien — local; trustworthy
  • For cheese: needs explicit hechsher (gelatin/rennet issue). Don't trust "vegetarian" labels.
  • For wine: needs explicit Mevushal/regular kosher hechsher. The Spar on Linzergasse will not have any — bring kiddush wine from Israel or order via Chabad/Kosher Tirol.

Kosher Tirol — Austria's kosher distributor

Verified real (koshertirol.com · info@koshertirol.com). They distribute basic kosher products — milk, yogurt, cheese, dips, bread, wine, grape juice, frozen meat, ready meals — to Mpreis chain stores across Tirol + Salzburg state and to "selected Spar" stores. Their summer-services page lists 15 Mpreis locations; none are in Salzburg city itself (closest is Altenmarkt and Flachau, ~45-75 min south). They also deliver anywhere in Austria. Email info@koshertirol.com ~2 weeks ahead to ask: (a) which Salzburg-city Spar (if any) carries their summer 2026 kosher section, and (b) whether they'll deliver kiddush wine + frozen kosher meat to the master Linzergasse apartment for Friday-afternoon arrival.

Layer 3 — Chabad meals (the win)

  • Friday-night dinner at the Gruzman table — the social anchor.
  • Shabbat-day kiddush + lunch after morning davening.
  • Or Shabbat Meals To-Go via Flowiz — order ahead, pick up Friday afternoon, eat at the apartment if low-social-energy. Both options work.

Bakery note: v1 of this page mentioned "Mann's Bakery on Linzergasse" for challah — cannot verify that bakery exists. Treating as v1 fabrication. The reliable challah path is: ask Chabad to add 2 challot to the meal order, or bring 2 sealed-packaged challot from Israel in the suitcase (Angel's / Berman's vacuum-sealed travel fine).

No eruv. No mikveh. Plan accordingly.

Chabad of Salzburg's own FAQ, verbatim: "There is no Eruv in Salzburg" and "Unfortunately, there are currently no active Mikvahs in Salzburg."

Practical consequences

  • Don't carry anything in public on Shabbat — no siddur in a purse, no tissues in a pocket if you're being strict, no keys (leave one inside the door, use a doorstop, or coordinate with host).
  • Wear, don't carry: sweater wrapped around shoulders is wearing; pushing the apartment key inside a sewn-closed shabbat-belt is wearing. Carrying a phone is carrying.
  • Apartment key trick: tell the host you'll be in/out throughout Shabbat. Either leave the door unlocked (master Linzergasse has a secure building entrance — apartment unlocked is usually fine) or arrange a hidden spot. Confirm with the apartment Friday afternoon.
  • Apartment-to-Chabad route is fully within a private domain transition? No — you'd walk public street, public sidewalk. Be carry-aware.
  • If you want to be lenient: Chabad-style poskim often treat a closed pocket with a non-essential item differently from a real "carry." Ask the rabbi if it matters to you. v1 of this page suggested asking; that's still the right move.

Saturday afternoon — what to do with the daylight

Chabad lunch ends ~14:30. Long European twilight gives ~6 hours of daylight before Havdalah (21:49). Three Shabbat-legal shapes — pick what fits the energy:

🌿 Recommended combo · the Mönchsberg ridge walk

Why this fits: Mönchsberg from Toscaninihof is the most beautiful walk in Salzburg and 100% Shabbat-legal (stone stairs, no electric lift). Ends with the Salzach sunset that the whole trip is partly about. Long break between lunch + walk = recovery.

  • Nap at apartment 15:00-17:00.
  • 17:30: Walk across the Staatsbrücke to Toscaninihof, up Mönchsberg via the stone stairs. ~15-min ascent.
  • 18:00: Ridge walk to Modern Art Museum terrace — viewing balcony only, no entry needed. City + fortress view.
  • 19:30: Slow descent via Festungsgasse path. Walk through the old town as gold-hour light hits the buildings.
  • 20:52: Sunset from the Salzach river bank near Makartsteg footbridge.
  • 21:49: Havdalah at apartment.

🛋 Or instead · soft afternoon if jet lag won the morning

Stay close to the apartment. Mirabell Gardens bench (6 min away, all flat), Salzach river walk both directions, skip the Mönchsberg climb. Sunset from the river right at the Makartsteg footbridge instead of from above. Same Havdalah, less elevation.

📖 Or instead · home afternoon, walk only at golden hour

Long Shabbat nap + reading at the apartment until ~19:30. One short walk to the river for the sunset (3 min from apartment), back for Havdalah. The "we landed yesterday and we're still tired" option — totally legitimate.

Carry rules apply to all three: no carrying outside the apartment. Apartment key arrangement made Friday before Shabbat. Cash for any non-Shabbat post-Havdalah purchase already pre-located.

Jewish historical sites (post-Shabbat / for context)

Salzburg's pre-Holocaust community was destroyed in 1938. Today ~100 Jews. Worth knowing about, even if you don't visit:

  • Salzburg Synagogue (IKG) · Lasserstrasse 8 — original 1901 building, destroyed Nov 1938, rebuilt 1967-68 with restored historic façade. Currently active under IKG.
  • Jewish cemetery · Aigen district, southeast of city. Contact IKG (+43 662 87 2228) for access — usually closed; the office will arrange a visit.
  • Stolpersteine — bronze sidewalk markers for Jews murdered in the Holocaust, scattered throughout the old town and Andräviertel. You'll see them on the walk between apartment and Chabad.
