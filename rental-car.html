Cross-border verification — fail-loud notes

Germany + Slovenia per provider

Sixt publishes a clear two-zone schedule (verified from their AT terms page): Germany = Zone I (€11.90/day, capped €39.95) and Slovenia = Zone II (€16.50/day, capped €64.40). Both pre-authorisable at booking. This is the cleanest cross-border policy on the page.

Hertz, Avis, Europcar, Enterprise: all four typically permit Germany on Salzburg-airport rentals by default (the German border is 2 km from SZG). Slovenia is more variable — usually allowed for an extra cross-border fee on Standard/Compact class, sometimes restricted on Premium/Luxury. Their websites do not publish a consolidated schedule like Sixt does — you have to ask the counter.

Aggregator listings (DiscoverCars, Kayak, EconomyBookings, Wisecars, Sicily By Car, Dryyve, WheeGo, Buchbinder, Megadrive, Alamo at SZG): cross-border policies are set by the underlying supplier, not the aggregator. The aggregator shows you the price but the rules don't surface until you read the fine-print PDF at booking OR ask at the counter. Treat every aggregator listing as "DE likely yes, SI confirm at counter." Get it in writing on the contract before driving south.

What I could NOT verify: exact 2026 Slovenia cross-border fees for DiscoverCars-listed brokers (Dryyve, WheeGo, Sicily By Car), exact Hertz / Avis / Europcar Slovenia fees from their public websites. These vary by car class and need a live booking quote or a counter conversation. Don't drive into Slovenia until the contract explicitly authorises it.

The caveats — read these before booking

Things that aren't in the headline price

Vignette (Austrian motorway toll sticker)

Austrian motorways require a vignette — ~€11.50 for a 10-day sticker (2026 rate). Most rentals out of Salzburg airport include a digital vignette pre-affixed, but it's not automatic — confirm at the counter. If yours doesn't include it, buy a 10-day digital vignette from asfinag.at before driving onto the A1 / A10. Note: the stretch from the German border at Walserberg to Salzburg-Nord exit is vignette-exempt — the airport-to-old-town leg itself is free, but everything beyond Salzburg-Nord requires it (Hallstatt, Werfen, etc.).

Grossglockner High Alpine Road — separate toll (~€42 / 2026)

The Grossglockner Hochalpenstrasse is a private toll road, not on the vignette. Day ticket ~€42 per car as of 2026 (verify at grossglockner.at). Not in the current itinerary — but if you swap a day for it, that's a separate cash line.

International Driving Permit (IDP)

Allison drives on an Israeli license. Austria + Germany + Slovenia all technically recommend an IDP alongside the home-country license for rentals. The IDP is free in Israel via MEMSI — pick it up before the trip. Without it, most counters will rent anyway but it removes a refusal risk on arrival.

Fuel policy

Ask for full-to-full (return with a full tank). The full-to-empty option looks cheaper on paper but the per-litre markup is brutal. Last gas station before SZG on the way back is on the B1 / A1 ring road, ~3 min before the rental return.

Pickup hours Friday morning

Salzburg airport rental desks open 05:30-06:00 most days; flight lands 08:00, so the counter is open by then. Sixt + Hertz + Europcar + Avis + Enterprise all in-terminal — no shuttle. DiscoverCars-listed brokers (Megadrive, Buchbinder, Goldcar, Sicily By Car) are mostly shuttle pickups — add 10-15 minutes from terminal to keys.

Drop-off Friday morning at 05:00

5am drop-off for the early flight — confirm the return lot has 24-hour access (most do at SZG; you leave the keys in a drop-box). Pre-fill a return-receipt photo: take a time-stamped phone photo of the fuel gauge + odometer + a quick walk-around at drop-off so any phantom-damage claims later can be disputed.
