The caveats — read these before booking

Things that aren't in the headline price

Vignette (Austrian motorway toll sticker)

Austrian motorways require a vignette — ~€11.50 for a 10-day sticker (2026 rate). Most rentals out of Salzburg airport include a digital vignette pre-affixed, but it's not automatic — confirm at the counter. If yours doesn't include it, buy a 10-day digital vignette from asfinag.at before driving onto the A1 / A10. Note: the stretch from the German border at Walserberg to the Salzburg-Nord exit is vignette-exempt — so the airport-to-old-town leg itself is free, but everything beyond Salzburg-Nord requires it (Hallstatt, Werfen, etc.).

Grossglockner High Alpine Road — separate toll (~€42 / 2026)

The Grossglockner Hochalpenstrasse is a private toll road, not on the vignette. Day ticket ~€42 per car as of 2026 (verify at grossglockner.at). Not in the current itinerary — but if you swap a day for it, that's a separate cash line. The current plan stays on Königssee + Werfen + Schafberg, which need only the standard vignette.

Cross-border to Germany

Tuesday Jul 28 is Königssee in Berchtesgaden — that's Germany. Confirm cross-border-to-Germany allowed at the counter before driving off the lot. Sixt: tell them at booking, no fee. Europcar: pay a small cross-border fee (~€10-25). Hertz: included on most rentals. Most Salzburg-airport rentals allow Germany by default because SZG is 2 km from the German border, but never assume — confirm and get it in writing on the contract.

International Driving Permit (IDP)

Allison drives on an Israeli license. Austria + Germany both technically recommend an IDP alongside the home-country license for rentals. The IDP is free to issue in Israel via MEMSI — pick it up before the trip. Without it, most counters will rent anyway but it removes a refusal risk on arrival.

Insurance (CDW + theft)

Headline prices on this page typically include CDW + theft. There's usually a deductible / "excess" of €500-1,500 that you can buy down with an extra ~€10-15/day at the counter, or with a third-party policy (e.g. RentalCover.com, often €4-7/day) which is cheaper. Decide before pickup so you're not deciding under pressure at the counter.

Fuel policy

Ask for full-to-full (you return it with a full tank). The full-to-empty option looks cheaper on paper but the per-litre markup is brutal. Last gas station before SZG on the way back is on the B1 / A1 ring road, ~3 min before the rental return.

Pickup hours Friday morning

Salzburg airport rental desks open from 05:30-06:00 most days; flight lands 08:00, so the counter is open by then. Sixt + Hertz + Europcar all in-terminal — no shuttle. The cheaper DiscoverCars/EconomyBookings options on this page are mostly shuttle pickups (Megadrive, Buchbinder, Goldcar etc.) — add 10-15 minutes from terminal to keys.

Drop-off Friday morning

5am drop-off for the early flight — confirm the return lot has 24-hour access (most do at SZG; you leave the keys in a drop-box). Pre-fill a return-receipt photo: take a time-stamped phone photo of the fuel gauge + odometer + a quick walk-around at drop-off so any phantom-damage claims later can be disputed.
