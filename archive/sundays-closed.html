Sundays in Austria

Sun Jul 26 = everything closed (Ladenschlussgesetz). Move day Salzburg → Zell am See + post-Tisha-b'Av + needs Zell am See kitchen stock for the first leg (then again Tue Jul 28 for Gosau). Two working windows below — we'd lean toward the Saturday post-Havdalah sweep (lower pressure than scrambling Sunday morning), with Hauptbahnhof Spar as the backup if Saturday plans drift.

Austria still observes Ladenschlussgesetz — the federal "shop closing law." On Sundays, almost everything closes: supermarkets, drugstores, banks, most restaurants, all clothing shops. The fact is fixed; the workaround is your choice.

The one that matters: Sun Jul 26

Sunday Jul 26 is the move day — Salzburg → Zell am See (~1h20 drive). It's also the morning after Tisha b'Av ends (fast ends Saturday Jul 25 around 21:00), so the body wants real food. AND it's the day you need to stock the Zell am See apartment kitchen for the first leg (we'll re-stock again Tue Jul 28 when we move to Gosau — that's a weekday shopping day so easy).

Stocking the kitchen on Sunday with everything closed = no. You have two working windows:

  1. Saturday night after Havdalah (21:49) — what we'd lean toward. Fastest sweep at a late-open Spar near Linzergasse. Stock for Sunday + Monday minimum. Why: lower-pressure than a Sunday morning scramble, and the body wants food anyway after the Tisha b'Av fast that just ended ~21:00.
  2. Or instead: Sunday morning at Hauptbahnhof Spar. Open Sunday because it's in a train station (legal exception). The savior if Saturday night drifts. ~15 min walk from Linzergasse or ~5 min drive; works as the last stop before pointing the car south toward Zell am See.
The exceptions you can use

Sunday-open Spars within reach

Austrian law allows shops in train stations, airports, gas stations, and tourist zones to open Sundays. In Salzburg that gives you these:

Spar Hauptbahnhof

Südtiroler Platz 1 (main train station) · ~15 min walk from Linzergasse · ~5 min drive

Sundays: 06:00-23:00. Full Spar selection — produce, dairy with hechsher (check labels), packaged bread, water, snacks. The default Sunday move-day option. Park in the station's underground garage (€2/hr).

Maps + verify hours →

Spar Salzburg Airport (SZG)

Innsbrucker Bundesstraße 95 · airport terminal · ~10 min drive from Linzergasse

Sundays: typically 06:00-22:00 (verify on site — airport hours can shift seasonally). Smaller selection than Hauptbahnhof but works in a pinch. Convenient if your route to Zell am See passes the airport (it shouldn't — B311 south is the main route — but useful for the Thu Jul 30 airport-base re-stock).

Billa Stop & Shop · gas stations

Highway exits on A1 / A10 · ~20-min intervals

Many OMV and Shell gas stations on the autobahn run a Billa Stop & Shop or Spar Express — open 24/7 including Sundays. Limited selection (snacks, drinks, basic groceries) but you can pad the basket en route if you missed Salzburg.

Maps →

Tourist-zone shops (Zell am See, Hallstatt)

Village center · variable hours · Sundays often open

Zell am See's tourist core has a few small shops (kiosks, lake-side cafes) open Sunday afternoon in July. Hallstatt has similar lakefront grocers on the Gosau day-trip route. Don't rely on these as primary stocking — limited selection, no guarantees on hechsher.

The Saturday-night option

Spars open late Saturday for the after-Shabbat sweep

Havdalah is 21:49 Sat Jul 25. By the time you've made Havdalah, eaten something, and got to the car, you've probably got ~30 min before the latest Spars close. Aim for the Hauptbahnhof one — same place, no time pressure:

  • Spar Hauptbahnhof — open until 23:00 Saturdays. ~15 min walk from Linzergasse 76 (Chabad). Walk down Linzergasse, cross at Staatsbrücke, ~5 min more.
  • Interspar Alpenstraße — open Saturdays until 18:00 only (before Havdalah), so not usable for the after-Shabbat sweep. Listed so you don't waste time checking.
  • Hofer (Aldi) — closes by 18:00 Saturdays everywhere in Austria. Skip for this window.

Recommended sequence: Havdalah 21:49 → walk to Hauptbahnhof Spar 22:00 → stock for ~2 days at Zell am See (produce, dairy with hechsher, packaged bread, snacks) → back at apartment 23:00 → pack for Sunday move. Tuesday Jul 28's Zell→Gosau move is a weekday so re-stocking is easy en route.

What's closed besides supermarkets

The full Sunday list

Closed Sundays

  • All supermarkets except station/airport/highway ones
  • Pharmacies (one rotating "Notdienst" per district stays open — sign on door)
  • Banks + bureaux de change
  • Clothing stores, electronics, hardware
  • Hair salons, dry cleaners, repair shops
  • Most restaurants (tourist-zone ones do open — Salzburg Old Town, Hallstatt)
  • Post offices

Open Sundays

  • Tourist sights — museums, castles, churches, gardens
  • Cafés (most, especially Old Town)
  • Restaurants in tourist zones
  • Petrol stations (Spar Express / Billa Stop)
  • Train stations + Spar within
  • Salzburg Airport + Spar within
  • Notdienst-Apotheke (one rotating pharmacy per district)
Other Sundays on the trip

You also have Sun Aug 2? No — only one Sunday

The trip dates Jul 24-31 give you exactly one Sunday: Jul 26. That's why this matters so much: you can't course-correct by waiting a day. The fix is upstream (Saturday-night sweep or Sunday-morning Hauptbahnhof) — there is no downstream rescue.
