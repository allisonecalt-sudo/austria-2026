Salzburg for Shabbat. Lakes for the rest. Sunsets every night.

Nature-anchored, sunset-obsessed, together-paced. The Montenegro template ported to the Salzkammergut — one Shabbat base, one nature anchor, picnics on rocks, and a real plan for every day so we don't have to decide anything at 9am.

Why this plan

Two moves, three apartments, eight sunsets.

Land Friday morning in Salzburg and settle in for Shabbat five minutes from Chabad on Linzergasse. Sunday after Havdalah we drive east into the Salzkammergut and unpack into a balcony apartment in Obertraun for four deep nights — coffee in the same kitchen, lake out the window, day trips that fan out from there. Thursday we move once more to a quiet apartment four kilometers from the airport so Friday's flight is a ten-minute drive.

Every day ends at a named sunset spot with a real time. The Königssee evening boat is the Tara Bridge of this one.

"Sometimes I dangled my feet over the side of the bridge, other times I sat on the ground or stood, watching in awe. We stayed there for hours."
